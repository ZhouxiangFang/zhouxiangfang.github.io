About Me

I am a first-year CS PhD student at Rice University, advised by Prof. Hanjie Chen. My research interests mainly lie in Natural Language Processing (NLP), and aim to build more capable, reliable, and trustworthy AI systems to better assist human in various real-world applications. I currently focus on the intersection of training protocols and safety profiles of Large Language Models (LLMs). I obtained my Master degree at Johns Hopkins University, where I was honored to work with Mark Dredze and Daniel Khashabi. I obtained my Bachelor degree as an Outstanding Graduate at Zhejiang University, where I was also a student of the Turing Class, Chu Kochen Honors College.
